The Work

Buried in datasets, building models, translating financial questions into answers.

Most of my time is spent buried in datasets, building models, writing queries, and translating financial questions into answers a spreadsheet alone cannot give. My toolkit runs across Python, R, SQL, and Power BI, and my work tends to live somewhere between data analysis, financial modelling, machine learning, and visualisation.

I spent nearly four years at Nabil Investment Banking Limited managing mutual fund operations, which is where I first realised that the part of finance I loved most was the analytical side. That realisation eventually brought me to the UK, where I am now pursuing an MSc in Business Data Analytics at Bangor University and working on projects across credit risk, customer churn, and financial econometrics.

Outside of Work

Arguing, hiking, and analysing matches nobody asked me to analyse.

When I am not working with data, I am usually arguing about something. I am an active member of the Rhetorical and Debating Society, where the topics swing between serious socio-economic and political questions one week and oddly trivial ones the next, both of which I take far too seriously.

The rest of my time goes to evening workouts, short hikes around North Wales, post-match football analysis that nobody asked for, and a long-running fascination with history and geopolitics that I keep meaning to turn into something more than a hobby.
